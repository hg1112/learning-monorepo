# Pistachio Tres Leches Cake

> Adapted from [Well Seasoned Studio](https://www.wellseasonedstudio.com/pistachio-tres-leches-cake/),
> with garnish inspiration from [Honey What's Cooking](https://www.honeywhatscooking.com/pistachio-milk-cake-tres-leches/)

**Serves:** 12 | **Prep:** 40 min | **Bake:** 25 min | **Chill:** 4+ hours (overnight best)

---

## Step 0 — Make Your Own Pistachio Paste (since you have raw pistachios)

This replaces both pistachio butter and pistachio paste called for in the original recipe.

**Ingredients**
- 1½ cups raw unsalted pistachios
- 2–3 tbsp neutral oil (sunflower, grapeseed, or light olive oil)
- 1 tbsp powdered sugar (optional, for sweetness)
- 1–2 tbsp warm water (to loosen if needed)

**Method**
1. Dry roast pistachios in a pan on medium heat for 3–4 minutes, stirring constantly, until fragrant. Do not brown.
2. Let cool 5 minutes, then transfer to a food processor.
3. Blend on high for 4–5 minutes, scraping down the sides every minute.
4. Once it starts clumping, add oil one tablespoon at a time until smooth and paste-like.
5. Add powdered sugar if using. Blend another 30 seconds.
6. You should get roughly ¾ cup paste — enough for both the cake and the soak.

> **Tip:** The longer you blend, the smoother and more buttery it gets. Stop when it's
> a thick, spreadable consistency — you don't need it perfectly silky.

## Part 1 — The Sponge

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Cake flour | 1½ cups (210g) | Sub: all-purpose flour works, cake will be slightly denser |
| Pistachios, finely ground | ½ cup (65g) | From your roasted pistachios — pulse in food processor until sandy |
| Baking powder | 1 tsp | |
| Salt | ½ tsp | |
| Granulated sugar | ⅔ cup (133g) | |
| Eggs, separated | 6 large | Room temperature |
| Almond extract | 2 tsp | Sub: skip it, add extra vanilla — almond amplifies pistachio flavor |
| Vanilla extract | 1 tsp | |
| Cream of tartar | ¾ tsp | Sub: a few drops of lemon juice — helps stabilize egg whites |
| Heavy cream or whole milk | ½ cup | |
| Homemade pistachio paste | 3 tbsp | From Step 0 |

### Method

1. Preheat oven to **350°F (175°C)**. Grease a 9×13 inch baking dish.
2. Whisk together flour, ground pistachios, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
3. Beat egg yolks and sugar in a stand mixer (or hand mixer) for **5 minutes** until thick, pale, and ribbon-like.
4. Add almond extract, vanilla, pistachio paste, and ½ cup cream. Mix on low.
5. Fold in the flour mixture gently until just combined. Transfer batter to a large bowl.
6. In the clean mixer bowl, whip egg whites with cream of tartar until **stiff peaks** form (~3 min).
7. Fold egg whites into the batter in **three additions** — slow and deliberate, keep it airy.
8. Pour into prepared dish and bake **20–25 minutes** until the top is golden and a skewer comes out clean.
9. While the cake is still hot, poke holes **all over** with a skewer or fork — go deep and go dense.

## Part 2 — The Three-Milk Soak

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Sweetened condensed milk | 1 can (14 oz) | |
| Evaporated milk | 1 can (12 oz) | Sub: whole milk + 1 extra tbsp condensed milk |
| Heavy cream | ½ cup | Sub: whole milk, but cream makes it richer |
| Homemade pistachio paste | ⅓ cup | From Step 0 — this is the key flavor carrier in the soak |
| Cardamom, ground | ½ tsp | Highly recommended — elevates the pistachio flavor |

### Method

1. Whisk all soak ingredients together until the pistachio paste is fully dissolved and no lumps remain.
2. Pour **slowly and evenly** over the hot, poked cake — go edge to edge.
3. Let sit at room temperature for 30 minutes, then cover and refrigerate **at least 4 hours**, overnight preferred.

> The soak will look like too much liquid. It isn't. Pour all of it.

---

## Part 3 — Whipped Cream Topping

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Heavy whipping cream | 1½ cups (355ml) |
| Powdered sugar | 2 tbsp |
| Vanilla extract | ½ tsp |

### Method

1. Chill your bowl and whisk in the freezer for 10 minutes.
2. Whip cream, sugar, and vanilla to **medium-stiff peaks** — it should hold shape but still look lush, not grainy.
3. Spread evenly over the chilled cake.

## Tips for the Best Result

- **Overnight chill is non-negotiable** for that melt-in-mouth texture — the soak fully homogenizes and the crumb softens completely
- **Pour the soak while the cake is hot** — the heat opens the crumb and the liquid absorbs far deeper
- **Don't overbake** — pull the cake when just barely set, it will continue to cook slightly and will absorb more soak
- **Taste your pistachio paste** before using — if it's bland, add a small pinch of salt and a drop of almond extract to the paste itself

---

## Storage

- Refrigerate covered for up to **4 days**
- Do not freeze once assembled — the whipped cream topping will weep
- Add whipped cream topping **on the day of serving** if making more than a day ahead
