# Moab Anniversary Adventure Trip — Full Plan

**Dates:** June 1–10 (10 nights, full flex window)  
**Fly into:** Salt Lake City (SLC) → drive ~3.5 hrs to Moab  
**Base:** Moab, Utah — stay here the whole trip, everything is within 30 min

Moab is the adventure capital of the American Southwest. Red rock canyon country, the Colorado River, two national parks (Arches + Canyonlands), and more adventure operators than you can exhaust in a week. No swimming required for any activity on this itinerary.

---

## The Activities

### 1. Skydiving — Skydive Canyonlands
**What it is:** Tandem jump — strapped to a certified instructor the whole time. You freefall at ~120 mph for about 60 seconds, then parachute down for ~5 minutes.

**Why Moab specifically:** Jump altitude is up to 16,500 ft over Canyonlands National Park. You freefall with the Colorado River, Arches, and hundreds of miles of red rock visible in every direction. Considered one of the top 3 most scenic tandem drop zones in the US. The video footage from this jump looks unlike anything you've seen.

**Logistics:**
- Book well in advance — June weekends fill up fast
- Arrive ~1.5 hrs before your slot for training + gear
- Add the video/photo package — this is your first jump, you will regret not having it
- Cost: ~$250/person jump + ~$100 video package
- Weight limit: typically 220 lbs for tandem — confirm when booking
- Weather-dependent — desert winds can delay afternoon slots, so **book the earliest AM slot available**

---

### 2. Dry Canyoneering — Slot Canyon Rappelling
**What it is:** Guided descent into narrow slot canyons using ropes. You rappel down cliff faces into canyons sometimes only 2–3 feet wide, carved into smooth curved sandstone over millions of years. No water. No swimming. Just you, a rope, and a harness.

**Why it's special:** Slot canyons look like another planet — flowing sandstone walls, shafts of light cutting in from above, surreal colors. Rappelling into one is a completely different physical experience from skydiving. Two distinct adrenaline firsts in one trip.

**Logistics:**
- Guide companies: Desert Highlights, Moab Cliffs & Canyons, Dreamland Safari Tours
- Half-day or full-day options
- Cost: ~$100–150/person
- Wear closed-toe shoes you can get dirty, athletic clothes
- Full instruction provided — zero experience needed

---

### 3. Arches National Park
**What it is:** Over 2,000 natural sandstone arches in one park. Delicate Arch is on the Utah license plate for a reason.

**Best hikes (all before 9 AM or after 5 PM in June):**
- **Delicate Arch Trail** — 3 mi, moderate. Do this at sunset — the arch turns deep red-orange.
- **Devil's Garden Loop** — 7.9 mi, passes Landscape Arch (longest in North America) + several others
- **Park Avenue** — 2 mi easy walk, canyon walls that look like a Manhattan skyline in sandstone

**Logistics:**
- **Timed entry reservation required** — book at recreation.gov the moment you have your dates. June fills completely within minutes of slots opening (2 weeks before your visit date). This is the single most time-sensitive booking of the entire trip.
- Entry: $35/vehicle, valid 7 days
- Bring at least 3 liters of water per person — June hits 95–102°F midday

---

### 4. Dead Horse Point State Park
**What it is:** A mesa jutting out 2,000 ft above a horseshoe bend in the Colorado River. Arguably the best single viewpoint in all of Utah — better than anything in Arches or Canyonlands for sheer drama.

**Why it's a must:** At sunset the river far below turns gold, the canyon walls go orange and red, and you're standing on the edge of a cliff with nothing between you and the view. Budget 2 hours here minimum. Bring a camera.

**Logistics:**
- 30 min from Moab town, separate from Canyonlands (own entry fee ~$20/vehicle)
- No timed entry required
- Best visited at sunset

---

### 5. Canyonlands National Park — Island in the Sky
**What it is:** A flat mesa sitting 2,000 ft above the confluence of the Colorado and Green Rivers. You drive up onto it and the edges just drop away — canyon country stretching 100+ miles.

**Best stops:**
- **Mesa Arch at sunrise** — 15 min walk from parking. The arch frames the canyon below in a perfect frame. Golden light hits it at dawn.
- **Grand View Point** — the best 360° canyon overlook in the park
- **Upheaval Dome** — a strange geological crater, mysterious and worth the short walk

**Logistics:**
- No timed entry required (unlike Arches)
- 30 min from Moab
- Go at sunrise or late afternoon — midday heat is punishing

---

### 6. Off-Road Jeep Tours
**What it is:** Guided 4WD on trails that go over slickrock, down cliff edges, and through terrain a normal car would never survive.

**Best options:**
- **Hell's Revenge** — famous slickrock trail, wild angles, you'll feel the tilt in your stomach
- **Shafer Trail** — dramatic switchback descent from the Island in the Sky mesa
- **Fins & Things** — otherworldly sandstone fins, great for photos

**Logistics:**
- Rent a 4WD Jeep (Farabee's Jeep Rentals or Moab Jeep Rental, ~$250/day) — or book guided
- Guided options: Coyote Land Tours, High Point Hummer & ATV
- No off-road experience needed for guided tours

---

### 7. Hot Air Balloon — Sunrise Float
**What it is:** Drift over canyon country at dawn. No physical effort, zero adrenaline — pure visual experience. The desert at sunrise from 1,000 ft is a completely different kind of memorable.

**Why it fits the trip:** After skydiving and canyoneering, a balloon ride is the perfect contrast — slow, silent, romantic. You're floating over the same landscape you jumped into.

**Logistics:**
- Canyonlands Ballooning operates out of Moab
- Cost: ~$250–350/person
- Launches at sunrise — expect a 5 AM pickup
- Book well in advance, limited capacity

## Romantic Moments on This Trip

- **Skydiving together** — shared adrenaline is one of the fastest bonding experiences that exists. You'll be shaking and laughing together the second you land. Watch the video that night.
- **Dead Horse Point at sunset** — stand at the edge with the Colorado River glowing 2,000 ft below. Give it 2 hours. This is the most romantic moment on the trip.
- **Delicate Arch at sunset** — the arch turns deep red-orange. Take a photo together in front of it.
- **Under Canvas fire pit at night** — sit outside with total silence and stars wall-to-wall. Moab has some of the darkest skies in the country.

## Practical Notes

**Heat:**
- June in Moab: 95–102°F in the afternoon. This is real desert heat.
- Do all outdoor hiking and activities before 10 AM or after 5 PM
- Midday = rest in AC (hotel, car), rehydrate, nap
- Carry at least 3 liters of water per person any time you're outside
- Canyoneering and balloon rides are comfortable because guides account for heat in scheduling

**Skydiving:**
- Afternoon desert winds can delay or cancel — always book the first AM slot
- If weather scrubs the jump, you'll reschedule for the next available slot (this is why having flex days matters)

**Driving:**
- Rent a regular car at SLC for the drive + park visits
- Rent a Jeep separately for the off-road day (Farabee's or Moab Jeep Rental)
- Download offline maps before leaving SLC — cell signal drops in the parks and on canyon roads

**Food:**
- City Market in Moab is the main grocery store — stock a cooler before park days
- McDonald's, Subway, and a few fast-food options in town
- Pack snacks + water for every single outing, don't rely on finding food in the parks
