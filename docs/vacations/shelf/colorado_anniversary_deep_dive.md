# Colorado Adventure Road Trip — Full Plan

**Dates:** June 1–11 (using full flex window — 10 nights)  
**Route:** Denver IN → Denver OUT (full loop)  
**Drive total:** ~900 miles over 10 days (very manageable)  
**Note:** See [moab_adventure_deep_dive.md](moab_adventure_deep_dive.md) for the top adventure pick. This plan is the Colorado alternative — more variety of activities, longer road trip format.

---

## Adventure Activities in Colorado

- **Skydiving:** Mile-Hi Skydiving, Longmont (~45 min from Denver) — tandem, 14,000 ft, Rocky Mountain Front Range views
- **Whitewater Rafting:** Arkansas River through Browns Canyon (Class III-IV, great for first-timers) or Royal Gorge section (Class IV-V, more intense) — ~2 hrs from Denver
- **Via Ferrata, Ouray:** Iron rungs + cables fixed into a cliff face. Guided, no experience needed. One of the best via ferratas in North America.
- **Tandem Paragliding, Telluride:** Fly off the mountain strapped to a certified pilot, land in the canyon below
- **Jeep Tours:** Private off-road tours to alpine lakes and mountain passes

## Food on the Road

Mountain towns all have grocery stores and standard chains (Chipotle, Subway, etc.) in or near town. Stock a cooler in the car — it's the easiest way to eat on your schedule without worrying about reservations or weird menus. Walmart/King Soopers in larger stops (Steamboat, Aspen area, Durango) for resupply.

## Day-by-Day Itinerary

### Day 1 — Arrive Denver · Denver
- Land, pick up car (rent an SUV — some mountain roads are rough)
- Stay near Union Station — walkable, beautiful Beaux-Arts train hall
- Walk the 16th Street Mall at night, explore LoDo neighborhood

**Where to stay:** The Crawford Hotel (inside Union Station) — genuinely stunning property  
**Tip:** Denver is at 5,280 ft — drink a lot of water today to start acclimatizing before you go higher.

---

### Day 2 — Boulder + Drive to Estes Park · Estes Park
- Morning: Drive to **Boulder** (1.5 hrs) — walk **Pearl Street Mall**, grab coffee at **Boxcar Coffee**
- Hike the **Flatirons** (Chautauqua Park) — 2 hr loop, dramatic rock formations, easy trail
- Afternoon: Drive up **Boulder Canyon** to Nederland (cute mountain town), then over to **Estes Park** (~1.5 hrs total)
- Evening: Explore Estes Park — small walkable town right at the RMNP entrance

**Where to stay:** **The Stanley Hotel** — the actual hotel that inspired Stephen King's *The Shining*. Stunning mountain views, very romantic, ghost tours at night. Book the mountain-view rooms.  
**Tip:** Stanley Hotel fills up — book immediately.

---

### Day 3 — Rocky Mountain National Park · Estes Park
- **5:30 AM**: Drive into RMNP for sunrise over Bear Lake (10 min from Estes)
- Morning hike: **Bear Lake → Nymph Lake → Dream Lake → Emerald Lake** (~3.5 mi, iconic, stunning at sunrise)
- Mid-morning: Drive **Trail Ridge Road** — highest continuous paved road in the US, above treeline at 12,183 ft, elk everywhere
- Stop at **Alpine Visitor Center** (11,796 ft) — views stretch to Wyoming
- Afternoon: **Moraine Park** scenic drive, elk are everywhere in June
- Evening: Estes Park town walk, Stanley Hotel porch at sunset

**Tip:** RMNP requires timed entry reservations — book at recreation.gov as soon as dates are confirmed (~$2/person). Trail Ridge Road usually fully opens by mid-June; check nps.gov closer to date.

---

### Day 4 — Drive to Steamboat Springs · Steamboat Springs
- Easy morning in Estes, check out
- Drive over **Rabbit Ears Pass** (US-40) — gorgeous meadow and forest drive (~2.5 hrs), very underrated
- Arrive Steamboat midday — cow town turned ski town, charming and unpretentious
- Afternoon: Walk **Old Town Steamboat**, **Yampa River walk**, browse shops
- Late afternoon/evening: **Strawberry Park Hot Springs** — the main event

**Where to stay:** **The Steamboat Grand** or a downtown vacation rental  

**Strawberry Park Hot Springs:**  
Natural geothermal pools in a canyon creek, ~90°F water. After 5 PM it's adults-only. Go at sunset and stay into the night — soaking in a natural hot spring under a Colorado sky full of stars is genuinely one of the most romantic experiences available in the US. Bring just a towel. Entry ~$25/person. Drive up a rough dirt road to reach it — 4WD or high clearance recommended, or rideshare from town.

---

### Day 5 — Steamboat + Drive to Aspen · Aspen
- Easy Steamboat morning, optional second visit to Strawberry Park in daylight
- Drive to Aspen via **Glenwood Canyon** on I-70 — 12-mile canyon carved by the Colorado River, sheer 2,000 ft cliffs on both sides, one of the most dramatic interstate drives in the country
- Stop in **Glenwood Springs** — **Glenwood Hot Springs** pool is right there (massive, outdoor, great for a quick soak)
- Arrive Aspen mid-afternoon
- Evening: Walk **Hyman Avenue Mall**, window-shop, people-watch (Aspen is great for that)

**Where to stay:** **The Little Nell** (the splurge — ski-in/ski-out, best hotel in Aspen) or **Hotel Aspen** (solid, more reasonable)

---

### Day 6 — Maroon Bells + Aspen · Aspen
- **4:30 AM wake-up** — drive to Maroon Bells (timed entry required, book at recreation.gov)
- Sunrise at Maroon Bells: Two 14,000-ft peaks reflected in a lake with wildflowers. Most photographed spot in Colorado. Genuinely lives up to the hype. Bring layers — cold at dawn.
- Morning hike: **Crater Lake Trail** (3.6 mi out-and-back from Maroon Lake, moderate)
- Back in Aspen by 10–11 AM
- Afternoon: **Aspen Art Museum** (free, small but excellent) + **Roaring Fork River walk**
- Spa: **The Little Nell Spa** or **Remède Spa at St. Regis Aspen** — book couples treatment in advance

**Tip:** Late June has more wildflowers; early June has fewer people. Both are worth it.

---

### Day 7 — Drive to Crested Butte · Crested Butte
- Drive via **CO-133** over **McClure Pass** (~2 hrs) — most underrated scenic drive in Colorado, through canyon and forest
- **Crested Butte** = wildflower capital of Colorado. No mega-resorts, preserved Victorian main street, very laid-back
- If late June: wildflower fields on every hillside — carpets of color in all directions
- Afternoon hike: **Snodgrass Trail** (mountain loop, great town views) or **Judd Falls** (easy waterfall hike)
- Walk **Elk Avenue** — galleries, local shops, very unhurried pace

**Where to stay:** **The Grand Crested Butte** or **Elk Mountain Lodge** (Victorian B&B)  
**Tip:** Wildflower Festival is late June/early July — if dates overlap, there are guided wildflower hikes and photography walks, great for couples.

---

### Day 8 — Drive to Telluride · Telluride
- Drive from Crested Butte to Telluride (~1.5 hrs through Gunnison)
- Telluride sits in a box canyon — sheer 13,000 ft walls on three sides. One of the most dramatic settings of any town in the US.
- Afternoon: **Free gondola** up to Mountain Village — ride up, walk around at 10,500 ft, ride down at sunset
- Hike to **Bridal Veil Falls** (2.5 mi round trip) — Colorado's tallest free-falling waterfall, trail passes right behind it
- Sunset: Take the gondola back up — Mountain Village at golden hour with canyon views is surreal

**Where to stay:** **Hotel Madeline** (gondola-accessible, mountain views) or **The Peaks Resort** in Mountain Village (spa on site)

---

### Day 9 — Telluride Full Day · Telluride
- Morning: Walk **Colorado Ave** (the main street), coffee, take it slow
- Late morning: **Private jeep tour** — Telluride Outside or San Juan Outdoor offer 2-person private tours to **Alta Lakes** (three reflecting alpine lakes at 11,000 ft), **Imogene Pass**, or **Yankee Boy Basin** (wildflower fields). This is the highlight activity of the trip — budget ~$150–250, completely worth it.
- Afternoon: Town Park (canyon walls surround you), **Telluride Historical Museum**, or just sit and absorb the scenery
- **The Peaks Resort Spa** — one of the best mountain spas in the country, gondola-accessible. Book couples treatment in advance.

**Tip:** Mention it's your anniversary when booking the jeep tour — guides often customize the route and stop for photos at the best spots.

---

### Day 10 — Million Dollar Highway + Ouray · Ouray
- Drive **Million Dollar Highway** (US-550) from Ridgway to Ouray (~45 min)
- This is one of the most dramatic drives in North America: cliff-carved road, sheer drops, no guardrails on stretches, mountain passes over 11,000 ft. Do it in daylight, drive slow, stop at the pullouts.
- **Ouray** = "Switzerland of America." Population 1,000, ringed completely by 13,000 ft peaks. You pull into town and your jaw drops.
- **Box Canyon Lodge** private hot tub — geothermal-fed hot tub on your private balcony, looking straight up at the canyon walls. Do this in the evening.
- Walk **Box Canyon Falls** (15 min hike into a narrow slot canyon to a thundering waterfall)
- Walk the tiny town — it's charming and takes 20 minutes

**Where to stay:** **Box Canyon Lodge** — book the hot tub rooms. Private natural hot spring on your own balcony under the canyon and stars. This is the most romantic night of the trip.  
**Tip:** These rooms book out early. Reserve immediately once you commit.

---

### Day 11 — Durango & Silverton Train · Durango → Denver
- Drive Ouray → Durango (~1.5 hrs via US-550 south — more of the Million Dollar Highway)
- **Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad**: Train departs 8:45 AM, returns ~5:30 PM. 3.5-hour ride each way through the Animas River gorge — cliff-hanging narrow gauge tracks, 19th century wooden railcars, sheer canyon walls. Book the **Rear Open Platform Car** — open air, unobstructed views, best seat on the train.
  - Buy tickets at durangotrain.com well in advance
  - Bring snacks and layers (it gets cool in the open car at elevation)
- Evening: Drive back to Denver (6 hrs through the San Juan mountains and high desert) or fly DRO → DEN
- Depart next morning

## Practical Notes

**Altitude:**
- Denver: 5,280 ft → Aspen: 7,908 ft → Telluride: 8,750 ft → Trail Ridge Road: 12,183 ft
- Altitude sickness symptoms: headache, fatigue, nausea. First 1–2 days are the adjustment window.
- Drink far more water than normal. The itinerary naturally acclimatizes you by starting low (Denver) before going high.
- If bad headaches hit above 10,000 ft, descend — don't push through.

**Weather in June:**
- Dawn/early morning: 40–55°F above 9,000 ft — always bring a layer
- Afternoons: 65–75°F, sunny, perfect
- Afternoon thunderstorms are common from mid-June — finish exposed ridge hikes before noon

**Driving:**
- Rent an SUV — more comfortable on dirt roads, and some jeep-tour staging areas require it
- Download offline maps before leaving signal range (Crested Butte, Telluride, Ouray all drop signal)
- Million Dollar Highway (US-550, Ouray to Silverton): daytime only, no guardrails, dramatic drops — drive slow and enjoy it

**What to pack:**
- Hiking shoes / trail runners
- Fleece + rain jacket
- Sunscreen (UV is significantly stronger at altitude)
- Reusable water bottles — fill constantly
- One nicer outfit if you want to dress up for a gondola ride or spa day
- Camera / wide-angle phone lens for Maroon Bells + Ouray
