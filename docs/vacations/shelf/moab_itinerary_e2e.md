## Day 2 — Thursday, June 4 · RAFTING ✅

**Westwater Canyon, Colorado River — Class III-IV. First rafting experience.**

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 5:45 AM | Wake up. Light breakfast — eat something but not too heavy. |
| 6:30 AM | Drive to **Westwater Canyon put-in** (~40 min west on I-70) |
| 7:15 AM | Meet outfitter. **Safety briefing** — guide covers: life jacket use, what to do if you fall in (float on back, feet downstream, guide pulls you in). Life jackets stay on the entire time. Non-swimmers do this every day. |
| 8:00 AM | **On the water** — Westwater Canyon full day (~17 miles) |
| | Canyon walls of ancient Precambrian rock close in tight on both sides. Named rapids: Marble Canyon, Skull Rapid, Sock-it-to-Me, Last Chance. Calm stretches between drops where you float through 1,000 ft walls in total silence. |
| ~3:00 PM | Off the water, back in Moab |
| 4:00 PM | Shower, rest |
| 6:30 PM | **Dead Horse Point State Park** (30 min) — Colorado River 2,000 ft below at sunset |
| 8:30 PM | Back to camp. Early night — **tomorrow is skydiving.** |

**Cost: $488 total ✅**  
**Water:** Outfitter provides water on the river. Bring a dry bag for phone/camera.

## Day 3 — Friday, June 5 · SKYDIVING + Anniversary Night

**First jump. 16,500 ft over Canyonlands. Then celebrate.**

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 6:00 AM | Wake up. Very light breakfast — empty stomach is better for the jump. |
| 6:45 AM | Drive to **Skydive Canyonlands** at Canyonlands Regional Airport (CNY), 16 miles north of Moab on US-191 |
| 7:15 AM | Check in, sign waivers |
| 7:15–8:30 AM | **Ground training** — body position, altitude signals, what happens at deployment. ~45 min. Your instructor is strapped to you the whole time. |
| ~8:30 AM | Gear up, board the plane |
| ~9:00 AM | **THE JUMP** — 25 min flight to 16,500 ft. Door opens. 60 seconds of freefall at 120 mph over Canyonlands and Arches. Parachute deploys. 5 min float down. Land back on the airfield. |
| 10:00 AM | Watch your video together on the ground. You will be shaking and laughing. |
| 10:30 AM | Drive back to Moab |
| 11:00 AM–4:30 PM | **Rest** — adrenaline crash is real. Eat, hydrate, nap. |
| 5:30 PM | Easy sunset walk — **Corona Arch** (3 mi, 1 hr, stunning arch outside the parks, no reservation needed) |
| 8:00 PM | Return to Under Canvas — **anniversary setup in tent** (flowers + dessert pre-arranged). Sit at fire pit. Milky Way visible overhead with zero light pollution. |

**Cost:** ~$700 total (2 jumps + 2 video packages)  
**Note:** If winds cancel the jump, they reschedule to Jun 6 morning automatically. This is why Jun 5 is skydiving day — buffer exists.

## Day 4 — Saturday, June 6 · Arches National Park

**The most iconic park in the US. Beat the heat with an early start.**

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 5:30 AM | Wake up. Pack 2L water each + snacks. |
| 6:00 AM | **Enter Arches** (timed entry starts 6 AM — have recreation.gov QR code ready) |
| 6:15 AM | **Park Avenue** — 2 mi flat walk between 300 ft sandstone fins. Best light of the day. ~45 min. |
| 7:15 AM | **Balanced Rock** — quick stop, 3 min walk. Scale is hard to believe. |
| 7:30 AM | **Windows Section** — North Window, South Window, Turret Arch. Short walks, massive arches you can walk through. ~1 hr. |
| 8:45 AM | **Delicate Arch Trailhead** |
| 9:00–10:30 AM | **Delicate Arch Trail** — 3 mi, 470 ft gain. Last stretch is a narrow ledge on a cliff face. The arch at the end is surreal. Bring 1.5L water each. |
| 10:45 AM | Leave park — heat is building |
| 11:00 AM–5:00 PM | **Midday rest** — hotel, AC, food, nap |
| 5:30 PM | Return to Arches — **no reservation needed after 5 PM** |
| 6:00 PM | **Delicate Arch at sunset** — lower viewpoint (shorter walk). The arch turns deep red-orange. This is the shot. |
| 8:00 PM | Back to camp |

**Water:** No water sources inside Arches. Carry everything you need.  
**Arches timed entry tip:** Reservation is for Jun 6 entry window (6 AM slot). Set reminder for May 22 at 8 AM MT to grab it on recreation.gov.

## Day 5 — Sunday, June 7 · CANYONEERING + Canyonlands

**Rappel into slot canyons in the morning. Canyon overlook at sunset.**

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 6:30 AM | Wake up, eat breakfast |
| 7:00 AM | Meet **Desert Highlights** guide at their office (60 N 100 W, Moab) — they drive you to the canyon |
| 7:30 AM–12:00 PM | **Dry slot canyon rappelling** — 3–5 rappel drops into narrow slot canyons. Walls sometimes 2–3 ft wide, carved smooth by ancient water over millions of years. Shafts of light from above. Your guide teaches the harness, anchor, and descent technique. Zero water. Zero swimming. Completely different physical experience from the other two activities. |
| 12:00 PM | Back in Moab |
| 12:30–4:30 PM | Midday rest |
| 5:00 PM | Drive to **Canyonlands — Island in the Sky** (30 min) |
| 5:45 PM | **Grand View Point** — mesa edge, canyon country 100+ miles in every direction. Sunset light. |
| 7:30 PM | Back to camp |

**Cost:** ~$250–300 for both  
**Wear:** Closed-toe shoes, athletic clothes you can get dirty. Guide provides harness and helmet.

## Daily Heat Strategy

June in Moab hits 95–102°F from 11 AM–5 PM every day without fail.

- **Before 10 AM** → All hiking, rafting, canyoneering, skydiving
- **10 AM–5 PM** → Hotel/AC. Rest, eat, nap. Don't fight this.
- **After 5 PM** → Sunset hikes, overlooks, Dead Horse Point

Every day in this itinerary is built around this window.
