## Moab — 7 Days

**Fly:** SLC (Frontier GoWild) → 3.5 hr drive to Moab  
**Stay:** Under Canvas Moab (6 nights)  
**Heat strategy:** All outdoor activity before 10 AM or after 5 PM. Midday = AC rest.

| Day | Date | Plan |
|-----|------|------|
| 1 | Wed Jun 3 | Fly SLC → drive Moab → check in → stock cooler at City Market |
| 2 | **Thu Jun 4** | **RAFTING** — Westwater Canyon, Colorado River (Class III-IV, full day) ✅ $488 booked |
| 3 | Fri Jun 5 | **SKYDIVING** — 16,500 ft over Canyonlands (add video package) → Dead Horse Point sunset → anniversary night |
| 4 | Sat Jun 6 | Arches NP — Park Avenue, Windows, Delicate Arch (5:30 AM start) → evening return for sunset |
| 5 | Sun Jun 7 | **CANYONEERING** — dry slot canyon rappelling (morning) → Canyonlands Grand View Point (sunset) |
| 6 | Mon Jun 8 | Mesa Arch sunrise (5 AM) → Hell's Revenge jeep tour (morning) → pack up |
| 7 | Tue Jun 9 | Drive SLC (7 AM) → arrive 10:30 AM → fly home afternoon ✈ |

**Activity cost:** Rafting $488 ✅ + Skydiving ~$700 + Canyoneering ~$275 + Jeep ~$250  
**Total trip: ~$4,000–5,500**

**What makes it special:** Skydiving over Canyonlands is the best drop zone in the US. Slot canyons look like another planet. Arches is genuinely one of the most surreal landscapes on Earth.  
**The trade-off:** You lose 6–7 hrs of outdoor time every day to heat. Every activity has a strict early morning window.

---

## Colorado — 7 Days

**Fly:** DEN (Frontier GoWild) → drive into mountains  
**Route:** Denver area (Days 1–2) → Buena Vista/Arkansas River (Days 3–4) → Ouray/San Juans (Days 5–6) → DEN (Day 7)  
**No heat issue** — mountain towns are 65–78°F all day in June.

| Day | Date | Plan |
|-----|------|------|
| 1 | Wed Jun 3 | Fly DEN → drive to Golden/Colorado Springs (1 hr) → Red Rocks Amphitheater evening walk (free, stunning rock formations) |
| 2 | **Thu Jun 4** | **SKYDIVING** — Mile-Hi Skydiving, Longmont (14,000 ft, Rocky Mountain Front Range views). Drive to Buena Vista after (2.5 hrs) |
| 3 | Fri Jun 5 | **RAFTING** — Browns Canyon, Arkansas River (Class III-IV). Peak June snowmelt = powerful waves, best conditions of the year. Half-day or full day. |
| 4 | Sat Jun 6 | Drive to Ouray (3 hrs via Gunnison). Check in. **Box Canyon Falls** hike. Hot springs at night (Box Canyon Lodge — private geothermal tub). |
| 5 | Sun Jun 7 | **VIA FERRATA** — Ouray Via Ferrata (iron rungs + cables on cliff face, guided, no experience needed, one of the best in North America). Afternoon: **Million Dollar Highway** scenic drive to Silverton (sheer drops, no guardrails, stunning). |
| 6 | Mon Jun 8 | Drive back toward Denver (5.5 hrs — long but scenic through San Juan Mountains). Stop in Salida (cute mountain town, 1.5 hrs from Ouray). |
| 7 | Tue Jun 9 | Fly home from DEN ✈ |

**Activity cost:** Skydiving ~$700 + Rafting ~$300 + Via Ferrata guide ~$200 + Jeep/rentals ~$100  
**Total trip: ~$3,500–5,000**

**What makes it special:** 65–78°F means full days outside with no restrictions. Three completely different adventure activities (skydiving, rafting, via ferrata). Ouray is one of the most dramatic mountain towns in America. Box Canyon Lodge hot springs on your anniversary night is very romantic.  
**The trade-off:** More driving between locations (Day 6 is 5.5 hrs back to Denver). Skydiving backdrop less dramatic than Moab. No slot canyons or Arches.

---

## The Decision

**Choose Moab if:**
- The skydiving video matters — Canyonlands from 16,500 ft is a once-in-a-lifetime shot
- You're okay managing the heat with early starts
- You want to keep the rafting booking ($488 already paid)
- Arches National Park is on your bucket list

**Choose Colorado if:**
- Comfortable weather is important — being outside all day without rationing
- A road trip through mountain towns sounds appealing
- You prefer via ferrata over slot canyons as your 3rd adventure
- You're okay losing the $488 rafting booking (check cancellation policy first)

---

## One Thing to Check Before Deciding

**Moab rafting cancellation policy** — if you go Colorado, find out if that $488 is refundable or transferable. If it's non-refundable, that's a real cost of switching.
